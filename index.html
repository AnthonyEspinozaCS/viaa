A helping hand at home

Our Purpose

KeepingCare is on a mission to meet the evolving needs of Canadians who receive care in their homes. We aim to provide comprehensive, personalized, and reliable medical and non-medical services that promote independence, comfort, and well-being. By attracting and retaining the best healthcare professionals, implementing advanced technologies, and leveraging strategic acquisitions, we aim to create a profitable business model that enhances the quality of care and ensures our clients' satisfaction. We are committed to addressing the challenges of an aging population and working towards bridging the gap in capacity and access to high-quality home healthcare services.

Our Values

At KeepingCare, our core value is to provide exceptional and compassionate home healthcare services to our clients and their families. We believe in treating everyone with respect, dignity, and empathy, ensuring their well-being and enhancing their quality of life. We prioritize the highest standards of professionalism, integrity, and ethical conduct in our operations.

Our Vision

Our vision is to be the leading provider of home healthcare services in Canada, renowned for our unparalleled expertise, innovative approaches, and commitment to delivering exceptional care. We strive to shape the future of the home healthcare industry, setting new benchmarks for quality, technology, and client satisfaction. Our dedication aims to improve the lives of individuals in need of care while supporting their families and the community.

Geographic Overview

Our strategic acquisition plan for KeepingCare in Canada, our initial focus will be on British Columbia (BC); characterized by high fragmentation, Canada's home healthcare sector comprises over 4,000 private enterprises, with a significant majority being profit-oriented independent entities. Factors contributing to this widespread fragmentation include the relative ease of launching a home healthcare business and the pervasive requirement for such services, particularly in rural or isolated locales. Although home care falls outside the purview of the Canada Health Act, classified as an "extended health service," there is considerable variation in how provinces and territories manage their publicly funded home healthcare programs. This variation, along with targeted funding for specific disabilities, First Nation citizens, and veterans, accentuates the industry's dispersed nature. As a result, there are notable challenges to the efficient integration and effective coordination of care services throughout the healthcare spectrum. To effectively break into this market, KeepingCare must carefully navigate these disparities and adopt a highly localized strategy, starting with BC. Despite its inherent challenges, the fragmentation also uncovers opportunities for expansion and consolidation within this crucial healthcare sector.

His journey continued at the Pharmaceutical Division of Cardinal Health, where he served as the Gulf Region VP from 2003 to 2006.
